The adjudication of U.S. visas is governed primarily by the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) of 1952, as amended. For engineers and policy researchers designing eligibility engines, it is critical to understand the foundational bifurcation of the U.S. immigration system: Immigrant Intent vs. Nonimmigrant Intent.Presumption of Immigrant Intent (INA 214(b)): By default, the INA presumes every foreign national is an "intending immigrant" (seeking permanent residence). To qualify for most temporary visas (like B-1, B-2, F-1), the applicant must affirmatively prove they are not intending immigrants. Failure to do so results in a 214(b) refusal, which is a finding of fact that cannot be appealed.Dual Intent: Certain visa classifications (H-1B, L-1, O-1) are statutorily exempt from this presumption. These applicants may hold temporary status while simultaneously pursuing permanent residence.Admissibility (INA 212(a)): All applicants, regardless of visa type, must be "admissible." Common grounds of inadmissibility include health-related grounds, criminal history, security concerns, public charge (financial sufficiency), and prior immigration violations (unlawful presence).This report breaks down the eligibility logic (Tier 1), documentation/status rules (Tier 2), and processing architecture (Tier 3) for ten distinct visa categories, synthesizing regulations from the Department of State (DOS), U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), and the Department of Homeland Security (DHS).2. Family Green Card (Immediate Relative & Family Preference)Tier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The Family Green Card system functions on two distinct logical tracks based on the relationship to the petitioner. The core adjudication variable is the existence of a qualifying relationship and the availability of a visa number.1. Immediate Relatives (IR): Unlimited AccessDefinition: Spouses, unmarried children under 21, and parents of U.S. Citizens (if the citizen is at least 21 years old).Logic: There is no annual cap on these visas. If the relationship is proven and the applicant is admissible, a visa number is immediately available.Age Limits: "Child" is strictly defined as unmarried and under 21. Under the Child Status Protection Act (CSPA), a child’s age may be "frozen" at the time of filing Form I-130 to prevent them from "aging out" into a lower preference category during processing delays.2. Family Preference Categories (F): Limited AccessDefinition: Relationships that are not "Immediate," including:F1: Unmarried sons/daughters (21+) of U.S. Citizens.F2A: Spouses/Children (<21) of Green Card Holders (LPRs).F2B: Unmarried sons/daughters (21+) of LPRs.F3: Married sons/daughters of U.S. Citizens.F4: Siblings of U.S. Citizens (citizen must be 21+).Logic: These are subject to strict annual numerical limits. Approval requires not just relationship verification but also a Priority Date check against the Visa Bulletin.Financial Sufficiency (Public Charge - INA 212(a)(4))A critical "hard gate" for approval is the Affidavit of Support (Form I-864). The petitioner must accept financial responsibility for the beneficiary.Threshold: The petitioner's household income must equal at least 125% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines for their household size. (100% is acceptable if the sponsor is active duty military petitioning for a spouse/child).Implication: Even with a valid marriage, if the sponsor cannot meet this threshold (or use a joint sponsor), the visa will be refused on Public Charge grounds.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Status MechanicsConditional Residence (CR-1): If the marriage is less than two years old at the time of Green Card approval, the status is granted on a conditional basis for two years. The couple must file Form I-751 to remove these conditions 90 days before the card expires, proving the marriage is still valid and was not entered into for evasion of immigration laws.Work Rights: Upon approval of the Green Card, work authorization is unrestricted. While an Adjustment of Status application (I-485) is pending, applicants may apply for an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) via Form I-765.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsI-130 (Petition), I-130A (Spouse Supp.), I-485 (Adjustment), I-864 (Support).Fees (2026)I-130: $675 (paper) / $625 (online). I-485: $1,440. ProcessingConsular Processing: For applicants abroad (via NVC). Adjustment of Status: For applicants inside U.S.3. H-1B Work Visa (Specialty Occupation)Tier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The H-1B logic engine centers on the definition of "Specialty Occupation" under 8 CFR 214.2(h)(4). It is not enough for a job to simply require a degree; the nexus between the duties and the degree is scrutinized.1. The "Specialty Occupation" Test
To qualify, the position must meet at least one of four criteria :A baccalaureate or higher degree (in a specific specialty) is normally the minimum entry requirement.The degree requirement is common to the industry for parallel positions.The employer normally requires a degree or its equivalent for the position.The nature of the specific duties is so specialized/complex that knowledge required to perform them is usually associated with a degree.2. The Beneficiary's Qualifications
The applicant must hold the required U.S. bachelor's degree or a foreign equivalent. If the degree is in a general field (e.g., "Business Administration" without specialization), it may trigger a Request for Evidence (RFE) or denial.3. Labor Condition Application (LCA)Before filing with USCIS, the employer must obtain a certified LCA from the Department of Labor.Wage Logic: The employer must attest to paying the higher of the "Actual Wage" (paid to similar employees) or the "Prevailing Wage" (determined by DOL data for the geo-location). This prevents wage suppression.4. Cap-Subject LogicUnless exempt (e.g., universities, non-profit research entities), petitions are subject to an annual cap of 65,000 (regular) + 20,000 (U.S. Master's exemption). Selection is conducted via an electronic registration lottery in March.2026 Update: DHS has moved toward a "beneficiary-centric" selection to prevent gaming of the system by submitting multiple registrations for the same person.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Duration: Initial grant up to 3 years. One extension up to 3 years. Maximum total stay: 6 years.AC21 Extensions: The American Competitiveness in the Twenty-First Century Act (AC21) allows for extensions beyond the 6-year limit in two cases :Recapture: Time spent outside the U.S. can be "recaptured" to extend the date.Green Card Pending: If an I-140 petition has been filed and pending for >365 days, or if an I-140 is approved but the applicant is facing retrogression (waitlist), they can extend in 1-year or 3-year increments respectively.Portability: H-1B workers can change employers. They can begin working for a new employer as soon as the new, non-frivolous I-129 petition is filed (they do not need to wait for approval).Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsI-129, H Classification Supplement, Data Collection Supplement.Fees (2026)I-129: $780 ($460 for small/nonprofits). Asylum Fee: $600 (large), $300 (small), $0 (nonprofit). Fraud Fee: $500. ACWIA: $750/$1,500. Registration: $215. ProcessingPremium Processing ($2,965) guarantees 15-day response.4. B-1 Business VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The B-1 visa is strictly for business activities other than gainful employment. The adjudication logic focuses heavily on the source of remuneration and the nature of the activity.1. Permissible Activities
The Foreign Affairs Manual (FAM) defines acceptable B-1 activities to include :engaging in commercial transactions causing no displacement of U.S. workers.Negotiating contracts.Consulting with business associates.Litigating.Participating in scientific/educational/professional conventions.2. Prohibited ActivitiesThe applicant cannot receive a salary or remuneration from a U.S. source, other than an allowance for incidental expenses (meals, lodging). "Hands-on" productive work (e.g., a construction manager picking up a hammer) is forbidden.3. Immigrant Intent (INA 214(b))The applicant is legally presumed to be an intending immigrant. To overcome this, they must demonstrate strong ties abroad:A residence outside the U.S. they have no intention of abandoning.Clear intent to depart at the conclusion of the temporary stay.Refusal under 214(b) is the most common outcome and indicates a failure to prove these ties.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Duration: Admission at the port of entry is determined by the Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officer, typically for the time necessary to conduct business (often 1 to 6 months).Extensions: Extensions (Form I-539) are possible but scrutinized heavily to ensure the applicant is not working.Domestic Employees: A special subset of B-1 exists for personal/domestic employees of U.S. citizens residing abroad or nonimmigrant employers (H, L, etc.) who are visiting the U.S. temporarily. These specific B-1 holders can work but must meet strict contract and wage requirements.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsDS-160 (Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application).Fees$185 (MRV Fee).ProcessingInterview at U.S. Embassy/Consulate. Biometrics taken on-site.5. B-2 Tourist VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The B-2 is the default classification for pleasure, tourism, and medical treatment. The eligibility logic mirrors the B-1 but focuses on recreational intent.1. Permissible ActivitiesTourism/Vacation.Visits with friends/relatives.Medical treatment.Participation in social events.Enrollment in short recreational courses (not for credit toward a degree).2. Medical Treatment Requirements
If the stated purpose is medical treatment, the applicant must provide specific evidence to the consular officer :Medical diagnosis from a local physician explaining why travel is needed.Letter from a U.S. physician/facility expressing willingness to treat and detailing projected costs.Proof of financial means to pay all costs out-of-pocket (USCIS/DOS are vigilant against potential Public Charge risks).3. Nonimmigrant IntentLike the B-1, strict adherence to INA 214(b) applies. Applicants without a job, property, or family obligations in their home country are high-risk for refusal.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Duration: CBP typically grants a standard 6-month admission period, regardless of whether the planned trip is 2 weeks or 4 months.Prohibitions: Strictly no work and no full-time study. "Birth Tourism" (traveling primarily to give birth in the U.S. to secure citizenship for the child) is a specific ground for denial.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsDS-160.Fees$185 (MRV Fee).InterviewRequired for most applicants age 14-79.6. F-1 Student VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)F-1 eligibility is a multi-step verification involving the school, the government database (SEVIS), and the consular officer.1. SEVP Certification & Form I-20
The applicant must first be accepted by a Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) certified school. The school issues Form I-20, which acts as the certificate of eligibility. Without a valid I-20, no visa can be issued.2. Financial EvidenceThe applicant must demonstrate liquid assets sufficient to cover tuition plus living expenses for the first academic year as estimated on the I-20. They must also show a projected ability to fund the remainder of the program.3. Intent to Depart (214(b))
Unlike H-1B workers, F-1 students must maintain a foreign residence and intent to depart the U.S. Consular officers frequently deny F-1 visas if they suspect the student is using the visa as a pretext to immigrate permanently.4. English Proficiency
The school must certify on the I-20 that the student is proficient in English or is enrolled in English language training.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Duration of Status (D/S): F-1 students are not admitted for a fixed date (e.g., "until June 30"). They are admitted for "D/S," meaning they remain in status as long as they are maintaining a full course of load and complying with SEVIS rules.Employment Authorization:On-Campus: Allowed incident to status (up to 20 hours/week while school is in session).CPT (Curricular Practical Training): Work integral to the curriculum (e.g., internships). Authorized by the Designated School Official (DSO).OPT (Optional Practical Training): 12 months of temporary employment directly related to the major area of study. Authorized by USCIS (EAD required).STEM OPT: Students with degrees in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math can apply for a 24-month extension of post-completion OPT.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsForm I-20 (School), DS-160 (State), I-765 (USCIS - for OPT).FeesVisa Fee: $185. SEVIS I-901 Fee: $350. I-765 (OPT): $470 (online)/$520 (paper).EntryCan enter U.S. no earlier than 30 days before program start date.7. EB-5 Investor VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The EB-5 program creates a path to a Green Card through significant capital investment that stimulates the U.S. economy. The EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act of 2022 (RIA) significantly overhauled eligibility.1. Investment ThresholdsStandard: $1,050,000.Targeted Employment Area (TEA): $800,000. TEAs include rural areas or areas with high unemployment (150% of national average).Infrastructure Projects: $800,000.2. Job Creation RequirementThe investment must create or preserve at least 10 full-time jobs for qualifying U.S. workers within two years.Direct Investors: Must create direct W-2 jobs.Regional Center Investors: Can count direct, indirect, and induced jobs (modeled economically), making it easier to meet the requirement.3. Source of Funds (SOF)This is the most scrutinized aspect. The investor must prove the lawful source of the entire investment amount (e.g., salary, property sale, inheritance, gift). The "Path of Funds" must be traced directly from the investor to the U.S. enterprise.4. "At Risk" Requirement
The capital must be irrevocably committed to the enterprise and subject to potential loss. Guarantees of return or redemption agreements are strictly prohibited and will cause denial.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Conditional Permanent Residence (CPR): Approval of the initial petition (I-526) grants a "Conditional" Green Card valid for 2 years.Removal of Conditions (I-829): In the 90-day window before the 2-year card expires, the investor must file Form I-829. They must prove:The money remained invested ("sustained").The 10 jobs were created or will be created within a reasonable time.Concurrent Filing: Under the RIA 2022, investors already in the U.S. can file for Adjustment of Status (I-485) concurrently with the I-526, allowing them to get work and travel permits while waiting for the petition approval.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsI-526 (Standalone), I-526E (Regional Center), I-829 (Conditions).Fees (2026)I-526/I-526E: $11,160. I-829: $9,525. Integrity Fund: $1,000 fee for investors.Set-AsidesVisas reserved for Rural (20%), High Unemployment (10%), Infrastructure (2%).8. K-1 Fiancé VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The K-1 is a hybrid visa: nonimmigrant in classification but immigrant in intent. It allows a U.S. Citizen to bring a foreign fiancé(e) to the U.S. for marriage.1. Bona Fide RelationshipThe petitioner and beneficiary must show a genuine intent to establish a life together. "Sham" engagements for immigration benefits are fraud.2. The 2-Year Meeting RequirementThe couple must have met in person at least once within the two years immediately preceding the filing of the petition.Waivers: Waivers are available only for:Extreme hardship to the petitioner (e.g., severe medical inability to travel).Strict cultural/religious prohibitions against meeting before marriage.Note: Financial cost or inconvenience is not a valid ground for a waiver.3. Legal Freedom to MarryBoth parties must be legally free to marry (divorced, widowed, single) at the time of filing.4. IMBRA Limitations
The International Marriage Broker Regulation Act (IMBRA) limits repeat filings. If a petitioner has filed two or more K-1 petitions in the past, or one within the last two years, they must apply for a waiver.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)The 90-Day Rule: Once the beneficiary enters the U.S., they must marry the petitioner within 90 days.No Extensions/Change of Status: K-1 status cannot be extended. The beneficiary cannot change to another nonimmigrant visa (e.g., student or work). If the marriage does not occur, they must depart.Adjustment of Status: After marriage, the beneficiary files Form I-485 to become a Conditional Permanent Resident.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsI-129F (Petition), DS-160 (Visa), I-485 (Adjustment).FeesI-129F: $675. Visa Fee: $265.DocumentsEvidence of meeting (boarding passes, photos), Statements of Intent.9. E-2 Treaty Investor VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The E-2 visa allows nationals of countries with a commerce treaty with the U.S. to direct an enterprise they have invested in.1. Treaty Nationality
The investor must be a national of a country with a valid E-2 treaty (e.g., UK, Canada, Japan, Grenada). The enterprise in the U.S. must also be at least 50% owned by nationals of that treaty country.2. Substantial Investment (Proportionality Test)There is no fixed dollar minimum (unlike EB-5). The investment must be "substantial" in relation to the total cost of purchasing or establishing the business.Insight: A $100,000 investment might be substantial for a consulting firm (low capital cost) but insufficient for a manufacturing plant.3. Marginality
The business cannot be "marginal." It must have the present or future capacity to generate income more than enough to provide a living for the investor and their family. It should create jobs for U.S. workers.4. ControlThe investor must seek entry solely to "develop and direct" the enterprise. This typically requires >50% ownership or operational control.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Duration: Admitted for 2-year periods. Can be renewed indefinitely as long as the business qualifies.Essential Employees: Managers, executives, or employees with "essential skills" of the same nationality can also obtain E-2 visas.Spousal Rights: Spouses (E-2S) are authorized to work in the U.S. incident to status; they do not need to apply for an EAD.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsDS-160, DS-156E. I-129 (if applying from within U.S.).FeesVisa Fee: $315. I-129: $1,015 ($510 small/nonprofit).ProcessingUsually processed at Consulate. No USCIS petition required for visa issuance.10. L-1 Intracompany Transfer VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The L-1 allows multinational companies to transfer employees from a foreign office to a U.S. office.1. The One-Year Rule
The beneficiary must have been employed abroad by the qualifying organization for at least one continuous year within the three years immediately preceding admission.2. Qualifying RelationshipThe U.S. and foreign entities must have a specific relationship: Parent, Subsidiary, Branch, or Affiliate. Both must be "doing business" for the duration of the stay.3. Capacity DefinitionsL-1A (Executive/Manager): Managing the organization, a department, or an essential function. Supervising other professionals.L-1B (Specialized Knowledge): Possessing "special" knowledge of the company's product/service or "advanced" knowledge of its processes/procedures. This knowledge must be distinct from the general labor market.4. New Office L-1
If the U.S. office has been doing business for <1 year, the visa is granted for only 1 year initially. The petitioner must prove they have secured physical premises and have a business plan to support the executive/managerial position within one year.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Maximum Stay:L-1A: 7 years.L-1B: 5 years.Dual Intent: L-1 holders may pursue Green Cards without affecting their nonimmigrant status.Blanket L Petitions: Large companies (sales >$25M or >1,000 employees) can get a "Blanket" approval, allowing them to transfer employees without filing individual I-129 petitions with USCIS.Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsI-129, L Supplement.Fees (2026)I-129: $1,385 ($695 small). Asylum Fee: $600/$300/$0. Fraud Fee: $500.ProcessingPremium Processing available ($2,965).11. O-1 Extraordinary Ability VisaTier 1: Core Approval Logic (Eligibility)The O-1 is for individuals who have risen to the very top of their field. It is often used as an alternative to H-1B for high-achievers.1. The Standard of "Extraordinary"O-1A (Science, Education, Business, Athletics): Must show "sustained national or international acclaim." Evidence: A major award (Nobel) OR meeting 3 of 8 criteria (e.g., lower awards, membership in elite associations, published material about the alien, original contributions, high salary, judging others).O-1B (Arts): Standard is "Distinction." Must be recognized as prominent.O-1B (Motion Picture/TV): Standard is "Extraordinary Achievement." Must be outstanding or notable.2. Peer Consultation
A unique requirement: The petitioner must submit a written advisory opinion from a U.S. "peer group" (labor union or management organization) confirming the beneficiary's eligibility. Negative opinions do not automatically disqualify, but must be included.3. Event-BasedThe visa is granted for the duration of a specific "event" or activity (e.g., a tour, a project, a contract). It is not an open-ended work permit.Tier 2: Supporting Context (Status & Documents)Duration: Initial approval up to 3 years. Extensions allowed in 1-year increments to complete the event.Agents: O-1s can be sponsored by a U.S. Agent (instead of a direct employer) to work for multiple employers on an itinerary.Dual Intent: While not statutory like H-1B, regulations allow O-1 approval despite an unabandoned foreign residence (effectively "quasi-dual intent").Tier 3: Reference Notes (Processing)ComponentDetailFormsI-129, O Supplement.FeesI-129: $1,055 ($530 small). Asylum Fee applies.ProcessingPremium Processing available ($2,965).12. Cross-Category Analysis & ConclusionsIntent ArchitectureA fundamental driver of eligibility logic is the concept of intent.Single Intent (Nonimmigrant): B-1, B-2, F-1, J-1 (generally). Risk: High refusal rate under 214(b) if ties are weak.Dual Intent: H-1B, L-1. Benefit: Can pursue Green Card openly.Quasi-Dual Intent: O-1, E-2. Nuance: Must intend to depart, but seeking permanent residence is not strictly a violation.Burden of ProofIn all categories, the burden of proof rests solely on the applicant (or petitioner). The standard of proof is generally "Preponderance of the Evidence" (more likely than not). However, consular officers have wide discretion, especially regarding intent adjudications (214(b)), which are essentially unreviewable factual determinations.Fee Structure Trends (2026)The 2026 fee rule (G-1055) introduces a tiered complexity:Asylum Program Fee: A new surcharge on I-129 petitions to fund asylum adjudications ($600 for large employers).Employer Size Differentiation: Small employers (≤25 FTE) and nonprofits pay roughly half the base fee of large employers.Online vs. Paper: Online filing often receives a $50 discount (e.g., I-130).This report provides the technical scaffolding necessary to build a robust eligibility determination engine for U.S. immigration. By codifying these "Tier 1" logic gates and "Tier 2" documentary requirements, developers can automate the preliminary assessment of visa viability.
